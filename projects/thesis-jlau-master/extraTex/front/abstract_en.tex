\jlauFrontHeading{Abstract}

Taking Nozomi Sasaki as a public case, this demonstration thesis presents the complete typesetting workflow for a Jilin Agricultural University master's thesis, including the cover, declarations, abstracts, contents, chapters, figures, tables, references, appendix, author biography, and acknowledgements. Magazines, film and television, advertising, and social media are treated as interconnected sites where a public image is continuously produced and negotiated.

The sample combines public-material coding, stage comparison, and textual analysis. It divides the career narrative into four illustrative stages and uses a process diagram, a trend chart, and three-line tables to demonstrate common academic layouts. The discussion suggests that a cross-media image is shaped dynamically by professional roles, communication rhythm, and audience interaction. All numerical values are synthetic and included solely for template demonstration.
