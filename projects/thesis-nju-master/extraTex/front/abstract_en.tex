\njuAbstractTitle{Abstract}

This sample thesis uses the public career trajectory of Nozomi Sasaki to
demonstrate how the Nanjing University MEM template can support a complete,
readable, and visually rich thesis project without relying on private research
data. The case is suitable for a public demo because it contains clear stages
of modeling, acting, variety show exposure, public controversy, and social
media rebuilding, which together form a coherent example of cross-media career
transition.

The thesis organizes the case into four stages: modeling entry, screen
expansion, controversy shock, and platform-led restart. It then analyzes the
transition through three lenses: content supply rhythm, platform control, and
career resilience. The sample argues that Sasaki's transition was sustained not
by a single successful role, but by repeated adjustments in narrative channel,
exposure cadence, and self-operated content after crisis. For template users,
this public example shows how `thesis-nju-master` can present front matter,
multiple chapters, figures, tables, references, acknowledgements, and appendix
content in a single default `main.tex` workflow.
